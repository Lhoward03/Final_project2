The NES

The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) is an 8-bit home video game console that helped revive the gaming industry in the mid-1980s. It’s a rectangular, gray machine known for its front-loading cartridge slot, which works almost like a VCR—players push the game cartridge down to lock it in place. The system uses simple, box-shaped controllers with a directional pad, two main action buttons, and Start/Select buttons, a layout that set the standard for future game controllers. Despite its modest hardware by today’s standards, the NES delivered colorful graphics, catchy chiptune music, and smooth gameplay that brought characters like Mario, Link, and Samus to life. Its library of games, reliability, and approachable design made it one of the most iconic and influential gaming consoles ever created.

NES Peripherals

Zapper Gun

The NES Zapper is a lightweight, pistol-shaped light gun controller for the Nintendo Entertainment System, famous for games like Duck Hunt. Available in both gray and bright orange versions, it uses a built-in light sensor that detects timed flashes on a CRT television to determine whether you hit a target. Its simple point-and-shoot design made it one of the most iconic and recognizable accessories of the 8-bit gaming era.

NES Controller

The original NES “brick” controller is a small, rectangular gamepad with sharp edges and a simple, iconic layout featuring a cross-shaped D-pad, red A and B buttons, and Start/Select in the center. Its flat, boxy design wasn’t the most ergonomic, but it became one of the most recognizable controllers in gaming history. The later “dogbone” controller kept the same button arrangement but introduced a rounded, curved shape that fit more naturally in the hands. Inspired by the smoother feel of the Super Nintendo controller, the dogbone offered a more comfortable and modern experience while remaining fully compatible with all NES games.
